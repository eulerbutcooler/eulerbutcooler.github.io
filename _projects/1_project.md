title: Reaching Nash Equilibrium in Rock-Paper-Scissors
summary: <ul class="card-text">
<li>Visualised the convergence of 5 algorithms onto <b>Nash Equilibrium</b> for the game of Rock-Paper-Scissors.
</li>
<li>
Rock-paper-Scissors is a zero-sum two-player imperfect information game whose calculation of Nash Equilibrium is
computationally intractable. Nash Equilibrium is that system policy from which neither of the players would want
to deviate for payoff.
</li>
<li>
Implemented <b>Counterfactual Regret Minimization, Monte Carlo Tree Search, Deep-Q Network
Reinforcement Learning, Proximal Policy Optimization, Q-learning and Monte-Carlo Simulation</b> for
comparing their rates of convergence to the game-theoretic Nash Equilibrium.
</li>
<li>
Research paper under review in IEEE International Conference on Computing, Communication, and Intelligent
Systems
</li>
</ul>
